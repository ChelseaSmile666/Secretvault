# Maxwell Verdict — US v. Maxwell (20-CR-330)

> **Verified public record.** Jury verdict returned December 29, 2021, Southern District of New York. Judge Alison J. Nathan presiding. Trial began November 29, 2021.

---

## Verdict

| Count | Charge | 18 U.S.C. | Verdict |
|-------|--------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Enticing a minor to travel for illegal sexual activity | § 2422(b) | **Guilty** |
| 2 | Transportation of a minor with intent | § 2423(a) | **Guilty** |
| 3 | Sex trafficking conspiracy | § 1594(c) | **Guilty** |
| 4 | Sex trafficking of a minor | § 1591 | **Guilty** |
| 5 | Conspiracy to entice minors | § 2422 | **Guilty** |
| 6 | Perjury | § 1623 | Not Guilty |

**Verdict: Guilty on 5 of 6 counts**

---

## Key Evidence / Trial Facts (Per Public Trial Record)

- Four victims testified under pseudonyms or first names only
- Testimony covered abuse at [[Locations/358-El-Brillo-Way-Palm-Beach]], [[Locations/9-East-71st-Street-NYC]], [[Locations/Little-Saint-James-USVI]], and other locations
- Testimony covered period approximately 1994–2004
- Flight logs admitted into evidence — documented co-travel with victims
- Former assistants testified regarding Maxwell's role managing Epstein's household and staff
- Physical evidence included massage table, sex toys, and other items from Epstein properties
- Calendar entries and communications documented victim "massage appointments"

---

## Sentencing
- Date: June 28, 2022
- Judge: Alison J. Nathan
- Sentence: **20 years** federal prison + 3 years supervised release
- Fine: $750,000
- Institution: FCI Tallahassee, Florida

### Maxwell's statement at sentencing:
Maxwell stated she was "sorry for the pain that was suffered" by victims.
