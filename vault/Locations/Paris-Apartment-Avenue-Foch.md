# Paris Apartment — Avenue Foch

> **Final destination before arrest.** Epstein was returning from Paris to the United States on July 6, 2019 when he was arrested at Teterboro Airport, NJ. His Paris apartment on Avenue Foch was a base for European operations.

## Avenue Foch Context

- Avenue Foch is one of the most exclusive addresses in Paris — broad, tree-lined avenue near the Bois de Boulogne
- Historically associated with French elite and wealthy foreign nationals
- The 16th arrondissement is Paris's most affluent district

---

## The Arrest (July 6, 2019)

- Epstein was returning from Paris (from the Avenue Foch apartment) when his private jet landed at Teterboro Airport, NJ
- FBI and NYPD were waiting; Epstein was arrested on arrival
- The timing suggested his Paris stay may have been an attempt to avoid or delay arrest
- He had been aware of the Miami Herald investigation (November 2018) for months

---

## French Investigation

- After Epstein's death, French authorities opened investigation into his Paris activities
- [[People/Jean-Luc-Brunel]] — who operated modeling agencies primarily from France — was a key figure in the French investigation
- Brunel was arrested in Paris in December 2020 while attempting to board a flight
- Brunel died in La Santé prison, Paris, February 2022 — hanging, ruled suicide
