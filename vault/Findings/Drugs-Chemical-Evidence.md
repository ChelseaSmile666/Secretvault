# Drug / Chemical Evidence in Epstein Operation

---

## Scopolamine (Devil's Breath) — Key Document EFTA00008744

### The Emails
- DOJ file reference **EFTA00008744** — email chains from 2014–2015 discussing scopolamine
- Scopolamine (hyoscine) = powerful anticholinergic drug; at low doses causes amnesia and dissociation; at higher doses causes unconsciousness
- Colloquially: "devil's breath" or "burundanga" — documented use by criminals to chemically subdue and rob/assault victims
- The emails in EFTA00008744 discuss scopolamine in context that prosecutors considered relevant to the trafficking operation
- Full content of the emails not fully released publicly; referenced in DOJ 2026 document release context

### Pharmacological Effects
| Effect | Relevance to Operation |
|--------|----------------------|
| Anterograde amnesia | Victim cannot remember events while drugged |
| Suggestibility | Victim compliant, follows instructions |
| Dissociation | Victim unaware of full context of actions |
| No obvious incapacitation | Victim appears functional to observers |

## Drugging and the Blackmail Operation

The use of chemical substances serves a dual function in the operation (see [[Findings/Blackmail-Operation]]):
