# 2026 DOJ Epstein Files Release

> **Verified public record.** The US Department of Justice released over 3.5 million pages of Epstein-related documents beginning December 2025 and completing a major tranche on January 30, 2026. Released under the **Epstein Files Transparency Act** (passed by Congress November 2025). Material available at justice.gov/epstein.

---

## Background

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Legislation | Epstein Files Transparency Act (passed Nov 2025) |
| Initial release deadline | December 19, 2025 |
| Major release | January 30, 2026 |
| Volume | 3.5 million pages; 180,000 images; 2,000 videos |
| Material types | Emails, text messages, court documents, flight logs, bank statements, wire transfers, FBI interview summaries |
| Portal | justice.gov/epstein |

---

## Key Documents Released

- Email chains between Epstein and associates spanning years
- Text messages
- FBI investigation summaries and interview reports
- Flight logs and travel records
- Bank statements and wire transfer records
- Internal DOJ investigation materials
- Draft statements (e.g. Maxwell draft statement re: Giuffre, Jan 2015)

---

## Redaction Controversy

- Attorneys criticised redactions as "ham-fisted"
- DOJ accused of "hiding names of perpetrators while exposing survivors"
- Some documents show same name exposed in one copy, redacted in another
- Congressional investigation opened into missing/withheld pages related to Trump accusations (bipartisan — both Dems and GOP)
- NPR reported DOJ removed/withheld files related to accusations about Trump

## DOJ Warning on Document Integrity

- DOJ issued statement noting the tranche "may include fake or falsely submitted images, documents or videos"
- Specifically flagged "untrue and sensationalist claims against President Trump that were submitted to the FBI right before the 2020 election"
- This warning applies to unverified tips in the collection — does **not** apply to official FBI/DOJ investigative documents
