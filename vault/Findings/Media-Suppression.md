# Media Suppression — Epstein Reporting

> **Documented.** Amy Robach's ABC News story was killed in 2015 after network interference. This is documented in Robach's own words (leaked). Other patterns of editorial delay are documented in journalism. The full scope of suppression is broader and partially documented.

---

## ABC News — The Robach Tape (Most Documented Case)

### What Happened
- In **2015**, ABC News anchor **Amy Robach** conducted on-camera interviews with Virginia Giuffre and other Epstein accusers
- Robach prepared a full report including Giuffre's allegations, corroborating evidence, and details of the network
- ABC News **killed the story** and did not air it

### The Leaked Tape (2019)
- **Project Veritas** obtained and published leaked footage of Robach speaking candidly at what she believed was an off-camera moment at ABC studios (November 2019)
- Robach on the tape:
  - "I've had this [story] for three years... we had Clinton, we had everything"
  - Stated the story was ready and had multiple corroborating sources
  - Said ABC executives killed it: "The palace found out that we had it. And I think [ABC] also quashed it. Definitely people in high places"
  - Referenced pressure from Buckingham Palace as part of why the story was killed
  - "I tried for three years to get it on to no avail and now it's all coming out"

### ABC's Response
- ABC News denied suppressing the story; claimed it "did not meet our standards to air"
- The network fired the staffer who leaked the tape (Ashley Bianco) — though Bianco said she did not leak the footage
- Bianco was hired by Project Veritas
- The discrepancy between "standards" and Robach's account is unresolved

### Significance
- The story was ready in **2015** — four years before Epstein's 2019 arrest
- If aired in 2015, Epstein might have been re-arrested and potentially more victims protected
- The "palace" reference implies Prince Andrew pressure played a role

## Miami Herald — How the Story Finally Broke

### Julie Brown's Investigation
- Miami Herald reporter **Julie Brown** published the investigation that finally broke the Epstein case in November 2018: "Perversion of Justice"
- Brown spent years investigating after others had dropped the story
- Her investigation detailed the 2008 NPA's extraordinary scope and the victim cover-up
- The Miami Herald investigation directly led to Epstein's 2019 federal arrest

### What Made It Possible
- Brown at a regional paper — less subject to elite advertiser/social pressure than national networks
- Brown's dogged sourcing of victims over years
- The Miami Herald's willingness to publish over apparent pressure

## Other Documented Suppression Patterns

### New York Magazine / Vicky Ward (2003)
- Vicky Ward wrote an early profile of Epstein for *New York Magazine* (2003)
- Ward later revealed her original draft included sexual assault allegations from two women
- The allegations were **edited out** before publication at the request of Epstein's representatives
- Ward discussed this publicly in 2019 after Epstein's arrest

### CBS / NBC
- Multiple networks had Epstein-related reporting in development pre-2019
- None broke stories with the scope of what was known; various editorial decisions made

### The "Golden Parachute" Pattern
- Journalists who pursued Epstein stories experienced career setbacks or stories being spiked #claim
- Pattern noted by multiple media critics but difficult to document comprehensively
