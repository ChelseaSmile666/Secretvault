# Florida Non-Prosecution Agreement — 2008

> **Verified public record.** Federal non-prosecution agreement signed June 30, 2008 between Epstein and the US Attorney's Office, Southern District of Florida. Widely criticised as allowing Epstein to avoid federal prosecution. Became subject of congressional inquiry in 2019.

---

## Key Terms (Per Released NPA)

- Epstein pleads guilty in Florida state court to:
  - Solicitation of prostitution
  - Solicitation involving a minor
- Sentence: 18 months Palm Beach County Stockade; served 13 months on work-release
- Federal charges: **declined** (NPA)
- Co-conspirators: NPA extended immunity to "any potential co-conspirators"
- Victims: Not notified of NPA prior to signing (later ruled unconstitutional — see *Doe v. US*)
- Registration: Required to register as sex offender

---

## Controversy

| Issue | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Victim notification | Victims not informed of NPA before signing; held unconstitutional |
| Work release | Epstein allowed to leave jail 12 hrs/day, 6 days/week |
| Immunity clause | Extended to unnamed co-conspirators |
| Secrecy | NPA kept secret from victims for years |

---

## Court Ruling on Victim Rights Violation

- Case: *Doe v. United States* (SDFL)
- Judge: Kenneth Marra
- Ruling (February 2019): Government violated Crime Victims' Rights Act by failing to notify victims and consult them before signing NPA
- Remedy proceedings ongoing when Epstein arrested in July 2019

---

## Congressional Scrutiny

- Senate Judiciary Committee opened inquiry into NPA, 2019
- Alexander Acosta (then Labor Secretary) resigned July 19, 2019 amid scrutiny
- Acosta stated he was told Epstein "belonged to intelligence" (per reporting; Acosta denied this characterization)
