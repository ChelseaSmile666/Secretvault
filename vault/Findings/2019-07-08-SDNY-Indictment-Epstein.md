# SDNY Indictment — US v. Epstein (19-CR-490)

> **Verified public record.** Federal grand jury indictment unsealed July 8, 2019 by Southern District of New York. Document publicly available via PACER and DOJ press release.

---

## Charges

| Count | Statute | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | 18 U.S.C. § 1591 | Sex trafficking of minors |
| 2 | 18 U.S.C. § 1594(c) | Conspiracy to commit sex trafficking |

---

## Key Allegations (Per Indictment Text)

- From approximately 2002 to 2005, Epstein "sexually exploited and abused dozens of minor girls" at properties in Manhattan (NY) and Palm Beach (FL)
- Epstein and co-conspirators created a "vast network of underage victims" using a "three-step" process: (1) recruit, (2) groom, (3) abuse
- Victims were paid cash for "massages" which then escalated to sexual acts
- Victims were encouraged to recruit other girls ("recruiter" role)
- Epstein paid co-conspirators to recruit additional victims

## Outcome
- Indictment dismissed August 29, 2019 following Epstein's death in custody (August 10, 2019)
- No trial took place; no finding of guilt in this case
- Underlying conduct addressed through Maxwell conviction (SDNY 20-CR-330)
